## 6. Per-net routing

All four I2C nets routed as trunks on **L1 (bottom)**. Each bus net has three endpoints — using the trunk-with-branches topology where one endpoint is the trunk start (ESP32) and the other two are reached by branches off the trunk.

### `+3V3`  — L1
Trunk + branch (3 endpoints):
- `WireSegment((-30.00, -11.92), (5.00, -11.92), L1)`  — east from J1A.3 to bus column at SCD41 x
- `WireSegment((5.00, -11.92), (5.00, -17.00), L1)`    — south to J2.1
- `WireSegment((5.00, -17.00), (20.00, -17.00), L1)`   — east along J2/J3 row to J3.1

### `GND`  — L1
- `WireSegment((-30.00, -14.46), (7.54, -14.46), L1)`  — east from J1A.2 to bus column at J2.2 x
- `WireSegment((7.54, -14.46), (7.54, -17.00), L1)`    — south to J2.2
- `WireSegment((7.54, -17.00), (22.54, -17.00), L1)`   — east along J2/J3 row to J3.2

### `SCL`  — L1
- `WireSegment((-12.22, -14.46), (10.08, -14.46), L1)` — east from J1B.2 to bus column at J2.3 x
- `WireSegment((10.08, -14.46), (10.08, -17.00), L1)`  — south to J2.3
- `WireSegment((10.08, -17.00), (25.08, -17.00), L1)`  — east to J3.3

### `SDA`  — L1
- `WireSegment((-12.22, -17.00), (12.62, -17.00), L1)` — east from J1B.1 along bottom row to J2.4
- `WireSegment((12.62, -17.00), (27.62, -17.00), L1)`  — east to J3.4

No vias. No L2 segments.

## 7. Layer rationale

- **All four I2C nets on L1**: this is the naive single-layer plan; it ignores that the J1A trunks have to cross the J1B column on L1.
- The SDA trunk runs the entire bottom row of the board on L1 because both J1B.1 and the sensor pins it serves are on y = -17.

## 8. Risks-to-validate

The check_routing step MUST verify each of the following:

- **Trunk-through-foreign-pin collisions** (failure mode #1 from common job info):
  - `+3V3` horizontal segment at y = -11.92 on L1 crosses J1B at x = -12.22 — collides with **J1B.3** (net `J1B_unused_3`).
  - `GND` horizontal segment at y = -14.46 on L1 crosses J1B at x = -12.22 — collides with **J1B.2** (net `SCL`).
  - `+3V3` east-along-bottom-row segment from (5, -17) → (20, -17) crosses J2.2, J2.3, J2.4 (foreign nets GND, SCL, SDA).
  - `GND` east-along-bottom-row segment from (7.54, -17) → (22.54, -17) crosses J2.3, J2.4, J3.1 (foreign nets SCL, SDA, +3V3).
  - `SCL` east-along-bottom-row segment from (10.08, -17) → (25.08, -17) crosses J2.4, J3.1, J3.2 (foreign nets SDA, +3V3, GND).
  - `SDA` east segment along y = -17 from (-12.22, -17) → (12.62, -17) crosses J2.1, J2.2, J2.3 (foreign nets +3V3, GND, SCL). The (12.62, -17) → (27.62, -17) continuation crosses J3.1, J3.2, J3.3 (foreign +3V3, GND, SCL).

- **Same-layer parallel collisions** (failure mode #2):
  - All four bottom-row segments at y = -17 on L1 (parts of `+3V3`, `GND`, `SCL`, `SDA` trunks) overlap each other for substantial spans.

- **Via-in-pocket** (failure mode #3): no vias in this plan, so not applicable.

- **Edge-of-board escape** (failure mode #4):
  - The J1A column is the inner-column-relative-to-sensors. Its escape east is blocked by J1B on L1. This plan does NOT change layers, so the failure mode IS triggered.

- **Pocket-floor cut on top-layer segments**: no L2 segments, so not applicable.

## 9. Open questions

- Defaulted hole diameter to 1.0 mm, channel cross-section to 0.8 × 0.8 mm, via diameter to 1.5 mm, pocket clearance to 0.3 mm (from brief).
- Exact pocket y-offsets for SCD41 and BH1750 follow `code/cad/src/vitamins/substrate.py:243-253` formula (`base_y + depth/2 - header_body_width/2`); not recomputed here.
- This plan is intentionally naive for testing the `check_routing` gate; a passing variant would re-layer J1A trunks to L2 across the J1B column (see plant-caravan PR #38 for the branch-merge topology).
